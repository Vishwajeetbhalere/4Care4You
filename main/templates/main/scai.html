Smart Cane AI: Encouraging Mobility

The Smart Cane AI (SCAI) is a revolutionary device designed to enhance the mobility and safety of visually impaired individuals. Unlike traditional canes that rely on tactile feedback, the SCAI leverages advanced sensors, artificial intelligence (AI), and communication technologies to provide users with a comprehensive understanding of their surroundings.



Multi-Sensor System

Equipped with ultrasonic, infrared, and temperature sensors, the SCAI offers unparalleled environmental awareness. These sensors, integrated with the AI engine, provide real-time feedback on obstacles, potholes, and more.

Controller System

The SCAI implements a multi-controller system for efficient data processing. Dedicated controllers manage sensors, cameras, and data communication, ensuring optimal performance and responsiveness.

High-Resolution PTZ Camera

Featuring a high-resolution PTZ camera, the SCAI captures detailed images of the user's surroundings. The AI engine analyzes these images to identify potential hazards, enhancing user safety.

GPS Tracking & Locator

With built-in GPS tracking, the SCAI allows users to share their location with caregivers or family members. Geofencing capabilities provide additional security by alerting users when they stray from their defined path.

IoT Communication Channels

The SCAI supports multiple communication channels, including 4G/5G, Wi-Fi, and Bluetooth. This connectivity enables seamless integration with other devices and services.

Artificial Intelligence Engine

The AI engine processes sensor data and image inputs to provide detailed environmental analysis. It identifies obstacles and provides real-time feedback to users, enhancing their mobility and safety.

Mobile Application

The SCAI's mobile application provides users with audible feedback based on the AI engine's analysis. It serves as a crucial interface between the user and the device, ensuring a seamless user experience.

Smart Braille Buttons

In addition to audio alerts, the SCAI features smart Braille buttons for tactile feedback. These buttons serve as secondary alerts, ensuring that users are aware of their surroundings.

Server Application

The central server application manages data processing and communication for the SCAI system. It enables traceability of devices and users, enhancing overall system security and accountability.

Facial Recognition

SCAI uses AI techniques to distinguish between male or a female and also has facial recognition capability for stored images.









Our Approach

The Smart Cane Al represents a significant advancement in mobility assistance technology. With its innovative features and user-centric design, the SCAI is poised to revolutionise the lives of visually impaired individuals worldwide.
